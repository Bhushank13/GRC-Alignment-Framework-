# ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Gap Analysis & Audit Matrix

**Objective:** To conduct a simulated internal audit against the ISO/IEC 27001 Annex A controls, identifying compliance gaps, documenting current-state evidence, and defining actionable remediation plans to achieve certification readiness.

**Audit Scope:** Enterprise IT Infrastructure, Identity & Access Management, and Vendor Management operations.

| Control ID | Control Objective (ISO 27001) | Current State / Evidence Reviewed | Compliance Status | Identified Gap | Remediation Action Plan |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A.5.1** | **Policies for Information Security:** Policies must be defined, approved by management, and communicated to employees. | Draft versions of Access Control and Data Privacy policies exist on the internal intranet. | 🟡 Partial | Policies lack formal executive sign-off and tracked employee acknowledgement. | Finalize policies, obtain CISO signature, and deploy via HR portal with mandatory e-signatures. |
| **A.5.9** | **Inventory of Information and Other Associated Assets:** Assets must be identified and an inventory maintained. | IT hardware is tracked in disjointed Excel spreadsheets across regional offices. | 🔴 Non-Compliant | Lack of a centralized, automated Configuration Management Database (CMDB) leads to orphaned assets. | **Priority:** Implement an automated ITAM/CMDB tool to discover, map, and track CI lifecycles continuously. |
| **A.5.15** | **Access Control:** Rules for granting access must be established and documented. | Active Directory is utilized, but local admin rights are heavily distributed to standard users. | 🔴 Non-Compliant | Violation of the Principle of Least Privilege; users can bypass security agents locally. | Revoke local administrative privileges globally; implement temporary elevation tools (e.g., LAPS) for IT staff. |
| **A.5.18** | **Access Rights:** Access rights to information and systems must be securely provisioned and de-provisioned. | HR notifies IT via email for terminations, averaging a 5-day delay in access revocation. | 🟡 Partial | Manual offboarding process introduces high insider-threat risk from orphaned accounts. | Automate account de-provisioning by integrating the HRIS system directly with Active Directory. |
| **A.5.20** | **Addressing Information Security within Supplier Agreements:** Security requirements must be established in vendor contracts. | Recent contracts include security clauses, but legacy vendors lack "Right to Audit" agreements. | 🟡 Partial | Legacy SaaS providers are holding enterprise data without contractual security obligations. | Execute a contract addendum campaign for legacy vendors to enforce modern security SLA clauses. |
| **A.8.2** | **Privileged Access Rights:** Allocation and use of privileged access must be restricted and controlled. | Admin accounts require passwords, but Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is not enforced globally. | 🔴 Non-Compliant | High risk of credential stuffing/brute force on cloud root and administrative accounts. | Mandate hardware-token MFA (e.g., YubiKey) for all global administrator and root accounts. |
| **A.8.8** | **Management of Technical Vulnerabilities:** Info about technical vulnerabilities must be obtained in a timely fashion. | Monthly Nessus scans are conducted, but critical patches often exceed a 30-day deployment window. | 🟡 Partial | Patch deployment SLA is too slow to mitigate zero-day exploitation risks effectively. | Enforce a strict 7-day SLA for patching critical/exploited CVEs; integrate automated patch management. |
| **A.8.12** | **Data Leakage Prevention (DLP):** Controls shall be applied to systems to prevent the unauthorized disclosure of sensitive data. | No technical controls prevent users from emailing PII/PHI to external personal domains. | 🔴 Non-Compliant | Severe risk of regulatory fines (HIPAA/GDPR) due to accidental or malicious data exfiltration. | Deploy endpoint and email DLP policies to block regex patterns matching SSN, Credit Cards, and PHI. |
| **A.8.16** | **Monitoring Activities:** Networks, systems, and applications shall be monitored for anomalous behavior. | SIEM is deployed, but log retention is limited to 30 days due to storage constraints. | 🟡 Partial | 30-day retention is insufficient for forensic investigations of advanced persistent threats (APTs). | Upgrade log storage capacity and forward cold logs to encrypted cloud storage for a minimum of 1 year. |
| **A.8.30** | **IT Readiness for Business Continuity:** IT infrastructure must meet continuity and disaster recovery requirements. | Backups are performed nightly, but a full network failover test has not occurred in 2 years. | 🔴 Non-Compliant | RTO/RPO metrics cannot be validated without testing; high risk of prolonged downtime. | Schedule a full-scale Disaster Recovery tabletop exercise and technical failover test within Q3. |
