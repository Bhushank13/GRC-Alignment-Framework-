# Enterprise IT & InfoSec Risk Register

**Objective:** To identify, assess, and track critical IT, Security, and Compliance risks across the enterprise architecture. 
**Methodology:** Risks are evaluated on a 5x5 matrix (Likelihood x Impact) to determine the Inherent Risk Score. Residual Risk is calculated post-mitigation.

| Risk ID | IT/Sec Domain | Asset / Process | Threat | Vulnerability | Likelihood | Impact | Inherent Score | Mitigation Strategy (Action Plan) | Residual Risk | Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RSK-01** | IAM | Active Directory | Privilege Escalation | Excessive persistent admin rights assigned to standard users. | 4 | 5 | **20 (High)** | Implement Just-In-Time (JIT) access and Privilege Access Management (PAM). | 4 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-02** | IAM | User Offboarding | Data Exfiltration / Insider Threat | Delayed revocation of access for terminated employees (Orphaned Accounts). | 4 | 4 | **16 (High)** | Automate identity lifecycle management; sync HRIS directly with Active Directory. | 4 (Low) | In Progress |
| **RSK-03** | IAM | Cloud Console | Account Takeover | Missing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for root/admin accounts. | 3 | 5 | **15 (High)** | Enforce hardware-based MFA (e.g., YubiKey) for all cloud root accounts. | 3 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-04** | IAM | Service Accounts | Lateral Movement | Static, non-expiring passwords used for application service accounts. | 4 | 4 | **16 (High)** | Store service credentials in an enterprise vault with auto-rotation policies. | 4 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-05** | IAM | VPN Access | Brute Force Attacks | Weak password complexity and lack of account lockout thresholds. | 5 | 4 | **20 (High)** | Enforce 14-character minimums, MFA, and lockout after 5 failed attempts. | 5 (Low) | In Progress |
| **RSK-06** | Cloud Sec. | AWS S3 Buckets | Mass Data Exposure | Buckets misconfigured with public read/write access. | 3 | 5 | **15 (High)** | Implement Service Control Policies (SCPs) to block public access globally. | 3 (Low) | Closed |
| **RSK-07** | Cloud Sec. | EC2 / Compute | Malware Infection | Permissive network security groups (Any/Any rules). | 4 | 4 | **16 (High)** | Restrict ingress/egress rules to strictly required IP ranges and ports. | 4 (Low) | In Progress |
| **RSK-08** | Network Sec. | Wireless LAN | Traffic Sniffing | Utilization of legacy WPA2-TKIP or weak Pre-Shared Keys (PSK). | 3 | 3 | **9 (Med)** | Upgrade infrastructure to WPA3-Enterprise using 802.1X authentication. | 3 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-09** | Network Sec. | Internal Databases | Ransomware Proliferation | Flat network architecture lacking internal segmentation. | 4 | 5 | **20 (High)** | Implement zero-trust network access and micro-segmentation for critical DBs. | 5 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-10** | AppSec / API | External API Gateway | Data Scraping / DDoS | Lack of rate limiting and payload inspection. | 5 | 3 | **15 (High)** | Deploy a Web Application Firewall (WAF) with strict rate-limiting rules. | 5 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-11** | Data Sec. | Lower Environments | PHI/PII Breach | Live production data copied into QA/Dev environments for testing. | 4 | 5 | **20 (High)** | Mandate data masking, tokenization, or synthetic data generation for lower envs. | 4 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-12** | Data Sec. | Corporate Email | Accidental Data Leakage | No outbound Data Loss Prevention (DLP) controls. | 4 | 4 | **16 (High)** | Deploy DLP rules to block or encrypt emails containing sensitive regex (e.g., SSN, CC). | 4 (Low) | In Progress |
| **RSK-13** | Data Sec. | Workstations | Physical Data Loss | Laptops missing Full Disk Encryption (FDE). | 3 | 4 | **12 (Med)** | Mandate BitLocker (Windows) / FileVault (Mac) enforcement via MDM. | 3 (Low) | Closed |
| **RSK-14** | Cryptography | Web Servers | Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) | Servers supporting deprecated TLS 1.0/1.1 protocols. | 4 | 3 | **12 (Med)** | Disable legacy protocols; enforce TLS 1.2+ with strong cipher suites. | 3 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-15** | AppSec / SDLC | Code Repositories | Credential Compromise | Hardcoded API keys and secrets stored in source code. | 4 | 5 | **20 (High)** | Integrate automated secret-scanning pre-commit hooks (e.g., GitLeaks). | 4 (Low) | In Progress |
| **RSK-16** | Vuln. Mgt. | Enterprise Servers | Zero-Day Exploitation | Patch deployment SLA exceeds 30 days for critical vulnerabilities. | 4 | 5 | **20 (High)** | Establish an emergency 7-day SLA for patching critical/exploited CVEs. | 5 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-17** | Endpoint Sec. | User Endpoints | Malware Execution | Standard end-users operating with local administrator privileges. | 5 | 4 | **20 (High)** | Revoke local admin rights globally; provide temporary escalation tools if needed. | 4 (Low) | In Progress |
| **RSK-18** | Endpoint Sec. | Hardware Ports | Malicious USB Payloads | Unrestricted access to removable mass storage devices. | 4 | 3 | **12 (Med)** | Block USB mass storage via endpoint security policy; whitelist necessary devices. | 3 (Low) | Closed |
| **RSK-19** | TPRM / AppSec | Third-Party Software | Supply Chain Attack | Blind reliance on unvetted open-source libraries. | 4 | 4 | **16 (High)** | Mandate Software Composition Analysis (SCA) tools and Software Bill of Materials (SBOM). | 4 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-20** | TPRM / Cloud | SaaS Applications | Data Residency Violations | Vendors hosting sensitive client data outside of approved geographic regions. | 3 | 4 | **12 (Med)** | Enforce strict data residency clauses during vendor contract negotiation. | 3 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-21** | TPRM / SecOps | Vendor Integrations | Over-privileged Access | Third-party API keys granted full read/write access to production. | 4 | 4 | **16 (High)** | Apply Principle of Least Privilege; restrict API scopes to specific necessary endpoints. | 4 (Low) | In Progress |
| **RSK-22** | ITGC / Change | Production Env. | Unauthorized Modifications | Code pushed to production without Change Advisory Board (CAB) approval. | 4 | 4 | **16 (High)** | Hardcode CAB approval requirements into ITSM/Jira deployment workflows. | 4 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-23** | ITGC / SDLC | CI/CD Pipeline | Malicious Code Injection | Lack of Separation of Duties (developers can write and deploy their own code). | 3 | 5 | **15 (High)** | Restrict production deployment rights strictly to automated CI/CD service accounts. | 3 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-24** | BCDR | Data Center | Prolonged Downtime | Disaster Recovery (DR) plan has not been tested in over 12 months. | 3 | 5 | **15 (High)** | Schedule bi-annual DR tabletop exercises and full technical failover tests. | 3 (Low) | In Progress |
| **RSK-25** | BCDR | System Backups | Ransomware Destruction | Backups stored on the same network domain as primary systems. | 4 | 5 | **20 (High)** | Implement air-gapped, immutable backup storage isolated from the main domain. | 4 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-26** | SecOps | SIEM Infrastructure | Delayed Incident Response | Alert fatigue caused by un-tuned logging rules and false positives. | 5 | 3 | **15 (High)** | Tune SIEM correlation rules to prioritize critical Indicators of Compromise (IoCs). | 5 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-27** | SecOps | Log Servers | Inability to perform Forensics | Audit logs overwritten or retained for less than 30 days. | 3 | 4 | **12 (Med)** | Configure centralized log forwarders to retain security logs for a minimum of 1 year. | 3 (Low) | In Progress |
| **RSK-28** | Data Privacy | Customer Databases | Regulatory Fines (GDPR) | Inability to cleanly execute user deletion requests ("Right to be Forgotten"). | 3 | 5 | **15 (High)** | Engineer automated scripts to purge specific user IDs across all connected databases. | 3 (Low) | Open |
| **RSK-29** | Physical Sec. | Server Room | Hardware Tampering | Tailgating allowed; lacking adequate access logging to secure areas. | 3 | 4 | **12 (Med)** | Install anti-tailgating turnstiles and biometric locks with 90-day access log retention. | 3 (Low) | Closed |
| **RSK-30** | IT Asset Mgt. | Enterprise Hardware | Exploitation of Legacy Tech | End-of-Life (EOL) operating systems actively processing business data. | 4 | 5 | **20 (High)** | Upgrade OS to supported versions or isolate legacy machines on a restricted VLAN. | 4 (Low) | Open |
